<h2 style="color: #eaeaea; border-bottom: 1px solid #301414; padding-bottom: 8px; margin-top: 50px; margin-bottom: 25px; font-size: 1.4em; letter-spacing: 0.15em; text-transform: uppercase; font-weight: normal;">Overworld</h2>

<h3 style="color: #c4bfa5; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.15em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; text-transform: uppercase; font-weight: 500;">Scandere Lignum</h3>
<div style="font-style: italic; color: #70685c; margin-bottom: 20px; font-size: 0.9em; letter-spacing: 0.02em;">
    <span style="color: #9e2a2a;">"Scandere"</span> - Latin for ''"to climb."'' &nbsp;|&nbsp; <span style="color: #9e2a2a;">"Lignum"</span> - Latin for ''"wood."''
</div>

<p>The Scandere is a unique tree that generates naturally in the Overworld forest biome. Unlike ordinary trees, it is alive in a way that goes beyond biology; its wood spreads slowly underground, threading through soil and stone in search of nutrients. What appears on the surface as a modest trunk conceals a vast, growing network beneath.</p>

<h4 style="color: #9e2a2a; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 12px; font-size: 0.9em; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase; font-weight: 600;">Behavioral Mechanics</h4>
<ul style="list-style-type: none; padding-left: 0; margin-bottom: 25px;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 10px; padding-left: 15px; border-left: 1px solid #401414; font-size: 0.95em;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Growth (Ascendance):</strong> The Scandere Lignum Log does not spread; instead, it grows upwards reaching massive heights of 50 to 80 blocks tall. It sprouts horizontal branches and forms a majestic, dense canopy crown of Oak Leaves and hanging Vines at the top.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 10px; padding-left: 15px; border-left: 1px solid #401414; font-size: 0.95em;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Spreading Roots:</strong> Scandere Lignum (all-sided bark root-wood) spreads underground tick by tick, replacing solid blocks in its path. Spread speed is accelerated by the fertilizing presence of nearby adult passive animals.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 10px; padding-left: 15px; border-left: 1px solid #401414; font-size: 0.95em;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Vein-Shape Control:</strong> To prevent chunky, square blocky structures, Scandere Lignum roots will not spread to blocks adjacent to 2 or more existing Scandere Lignum blocks, keeping root networks naturally thin and serpentine.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 10px; padding-left: 15px; border-left: 1px solid #401414; font-size: 0.95em;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Symbiosis:</strong> The Scandere Lignum Log emits a passive aura that influences the surrounding environment. Nearby passive mobs are drawn toward the log, idling in a ring around it. Animals within range have an increased chance to enter love mode and breed. The log is the heart of the organism — the Lignum roots spread, but the Log sustains.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 10px; padding-left: 15px; border-left: 1px solid #401414; font-size: 0.95em;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Infestation:</strong> Deep within the root-wood, Lignum Vermis parasites often lay dormant. Disturbed blocks carry a <strong style="color: #9e2a2a;">10% (0.1) chance</strong> to release a Lignum Vermis when completely destroyed by a player or an explosion. While actively being mined, there is an additional, rare <strong style="color: #9e2a2a;">1% (0.01) chance per tick</strong> for the creature to violently splinter the block early and erupt outward.</li>
</ul>

<h3 style="color: #c4bfa5; margin-bottom: 12px; font-size: 1.15em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; text-transform: uppercase; font-weight: 500;">Ash-Rose</h3>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">A specialized flower placed exclusively by the Ash-Weaver. It serves as the primary tool for managing dangerous Splinters.</p>

<div style="background: #0c0b0a; border: 1px solid #2d1814; border-left: 2px solid #9e2a2a; padding: 20px; margin-top: 15px; margin-bottom: 30px;">
<ul style="margin: 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: square; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em; color: #9c9788;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">Holding one in either hand causes all nearby Splinters to <strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">stop moving entirely</strong>.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">Placing one on the ground has the same freezing effect on any Splinter within range.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong style="color: #9e2a2a;">Wilts over time</strong> when held, eventually crumbling into nothing.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;">Can be replanted on grass, dirt, deepslate, and any type of log or plank.</li>
</ul>
</div>

<h3 style="color: #c4bfa5; margin-bottom: 12px; font-size: 1.15em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; text-transform: uppercase; font-weight: 500;">Fractus Core</h3>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">A stark block of pure, plain white geometry that defies the natural visual laws of the world. Obtained exclusively from the anomalous Fractus entity.</p>

<div style="background: #09090a; border: 1px solid #1c1c21; border-left: 2px solid #5d5d6a; padding: 20px; margin-top: 15px; margin-bottom: 30px; color: #9c9788;">
<ul style="margin: 0 0 20px 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: square; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Anomalous Weight:</strong> Keeping it anywhere in your inventory dynamically modifies your physics based on the total item count. Each core in your possession reduces movement speed by <strong style="color: #9e2a2a;">4%</strong> and increases falling gravity by <strong style="color: #9e2a2a;">+0.017</strong> value points.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">Radiates a constant light level of 13, cleanly pushing back shadows without generating any heat.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">Possesses incredible fuel stability, providing exactly 13 hours of continuous smelting power inside a furnace.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong style="color: #9e2a2a;">Fractus Invasion:</strong> Simply existing near a core—whether it is inside a player's inventory, dropped on the ground, stored inside a nearby container, or placed as a block within a <strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">120-block horizontal radius</strong>—draws unwanted cosmic attention in the Overworld and Nether dimensions.</li>
</ul>

<div style="border-top: 1px solid #1c1c21; padding-top: 15px; margin-top: 15px;">
<h4 style="color: #eaeaea; font-weight: 500; margin-top: 0; margin-bottom: 12px; font-size: 0.9em; letter-spacing: 0.08em; text-transform: uppercase;">Invasion Event Phases</h4>
<ol style="margin: 0; padding-left: 18px; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em; color: #8c8779;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Escalation (Stage 1):</strong> The longer a core remains tracked nearby, the faster breaches occur. The base interval between invasions starts at 2 minutes, but scales down to a aggressive 45 seconds under max escalation. Discarding or destroying the core decays this tracking speed factor.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Breach Forming (Stage 2):</strong> After 10 seconds of nearby exposure, a thunderous crack sounds as a massive, 2.8-meter swirling volumetric cyan and blue cosmic vortex begins expanding nearby over a 7-second charging period.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">Arrival (Stage 3):</strong> The gateway stabilizes for 6 seconds, emitting high-energy electric arcs. It swiftly summons a staggered horde of <strong style="color: #c4bfa5;">3 to 7 Fractus entities</strong> (plus escalation bonuses) that instantly lock hostility onto the player before collapsing with an explosive shockwave.</li>
</ol>
</div>
