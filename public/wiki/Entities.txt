<h3 style="color: #c4bfa5; margin-top: 40px; margin-bottom: 12px; font-size: 1.15em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; text-transform: uppercase; font-weight: 500;">The Splinter</h3>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">The primary threat of the Backwoods. A gaunt wooden figure that hunts the player through extreme patience and persistence. Every Splinter is assigned a dynamic personality profile upon spawning—half behave as stealthy stalkers, while the other half act as pure pursuers that advance directly toward the player whenever unwatched.</p>

<div style="background: #0b0e0c; border: 1px solid #1b221c; border-left: 2px solid #709978; padding: 12px 16px; margin: 15px 0; font-size: 0.85em; color: #8c8779; font-family: monospace;">

<h4 style="color: #eaeaea; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">AI Personality Profiles</h4>
<ul style="margin: 0 0 20px 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: square; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em; color: #9c9788;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">'''Stalker / Hunt Personality:''' Cycles systematically through three behavioral phases. In its ''Silent phase'', it stands completely motionless, observing. In its ''Stalk phase'', it follows at a distance, breaking line of sight and pausing occasionally to watch. In its ''Hunt phase'', it pursues the player aggressively. Following a hunt, it retreats into a forced silent cooldown before repeating the cycle.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;">'''Angel Personality:''' Behaves more directly. It aggressively travels toward the player whenever unwatched and freezes instantly the moment the player establishes eye contact, featuring no phase cycling.</li>
</ul>

<div style="background: #0d0909; border: 1px solid #321616; border-left: 2px solid #9e2a2a; padding: 16px; margin: 20px 0; color: #cfbfa8; font-size: 0.9em;">
<strong style="color: #9e2a2a; text-transform: uppercase; font-size: 0.85em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; display: block; margin-bottom: 4px;">The Rage Mechanic:</strong> Both personality types freeze under direct sight. However, staring at a Splinter for too long causes it to enter a state of rage—overriding its freeze condition and chasing the player regardless of eye contact. Striking a Splinter lowers this visual threshold, making it enrage faster with each consecutive hit.
</div>

<h4 style="color: #eaeaea; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">Global Alert Radii (On Death)</h4>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">Killing a "woodbound" entity triggers an immediate reaction from nearby kin. When a woodbound entity dies, it alerts all other woodbound entities within a specific scan radius, forcing them into a state of rage (<code>isEnraged</code>) for a forced hunt lasting '''600 ticks'''. The alert radius scales dynamically based on world difficulty settings:</p>
<ul style="margin: 0 0 20px 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: square; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em; color: #9c9788;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">'''Hard Difficulty:''' The radius expands dynamically to match the server's player list view distance (multiplied by 16 blocks), defaulting to a massive '''64-block radius''' if uncalculated.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">'''Normal Difficulty:''' Fixed at a '''40-block radius'''.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;">'''Easy / Other Difficulties:''' Fixed at a '''24-block radius'''.</li>
</ul>

<h4 style="color: #eaeaea; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">Environmental Navigation &amp; Mitigation</h4>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">Splinters are highly adaptive. If a player builds vertically to escape, the Splinter will build blocks upward to follow. If the player crosses a ravine, it will bridge across. It actively breaks blocks separating it from its target, though harder materials significantly reduce its mining speed. At advanced rot stages, it loses all building and bridging capabilities.</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">Carrying an '''Ash-Rose''' causes a Splinter to halt entirely, and planting one nearby freezes all Splinters within its active range. The item wilts over time when held in an inventory slot.</p>

<div style="background: #0d0909; border: 1px solid #321616; border-left: 2px solid #9e2a2a; padding: 16px; margin: 20px 0; color: #cfbfa8; font-size: 0.9em;">
<strong style="color: #9e2a2a; text-transform: uppercase; font-size: 0.85em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; display: block; margin-bottom: 4px;">Dimensional Pull:</strong> A rare subset of Splinters (10% spawn rate) possess the capability to pull players directly into The Grain dimension upon inflicting damage, carrying a 10% trigger chance per hit.
</div>

<h4 style="color: #eaeaea; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">Spawning Brackets &amp; Exclusion Zones</h4>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">To prevent global entity over-congestion, Splinters utilize dynamic '''Exclusion Zones'''—safety cuboids around their spawn coordinates where other Splinters cannot generate. These zones compress as world progression time increases, causing threats to feel denser as time passes.</p>

<h4 style="color: #eaeaea; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">Spawning Mechanics &amp; Environmental Demands</h4>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">Blindspot Splinters generate under strict architectural constraints. They require solid base blocks beneath them; they cannot spawn if the supporting coordinate is empty, and in specific target biomes, the floor block must be capable of fully occluding light (<code>canOcclude</code>).</p>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">Their underlying exclusion zones alter drastically depending on the current biome:</p>
<ul style="margin: 0 0 20px 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: square; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em; color: #9c9788;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">'''Labyrinthine Grids Biome:''' Spawns with a rigid, high-clearance exclusion zone radius of '''192 Blocks'''.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">'''Pillar Thicket Biome:''' Spawns with a rigid, high-clearance exclusion zone radius of '''192 Blocks'''.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;">'''Uniform Grain Biome:''' Generates highly spread out, requiring a massive exclusion zone radius of '''1000 Blocks'''.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;">'''The Dead Grain Biome (Sub-Strata Dimension):''' Spawns strictly inside an altitude window between Y-levels '''162 and 318'''. Its active population profile compresses dynamically based on total ticks spent by the player inside the dimension (<code>sub_strata_time</code>), forcing severe compaction over time:
        <ul style="margin: 8px 0 0 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: circle; line-height: 1.6; color: #8c8779;">

            <li style="margin-bottom: 4px;">'''3,751 – 5,500 Ticks:''' Exclusion zone radius compresses to '''58 Blocks'''.</li>
