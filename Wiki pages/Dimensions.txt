<!-- INTRODUCTION -->
<div style="background: rgba(25, 20, 20, 0.8); border-left: 5px solid #d84315; padding: 15px 20px; margin-bottom: 30px; border-radius: 0 8px 8px 0;">
The '''Backwoods''' mod features an intricate web of alternate dimensions and sub-realms. Players navigate through terrifying landscapes, reality-bending architectural anomalies, and decaying remnants of lost worlds.
</div>

<h2 style="color: #ff7043; border-bottom: 2px solid #d84315; padding-bottom: 5px; font-size: 2em; margin-top: 30px;">Dimensions</h2>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE BACKWOODS
     ========================================== -->
<h3 style="color: #ffab91; margin-top: 25px; font-size: 1.5em; border-bottom: 1px solid #3e2723; padding-bottom: 3px;">The Backwoods</h3>
<p>The Backwoods is the primary dimension of the mod. It is an endless, fog-heavy forest built entirely from oak planks and dead trees. The sky is a permanent amber haze, casting a light that feels fundamentally wrong. This dimension spans three distinct biomes.</p>

<h4 style="color: #ffccbc; margin-bottom: 10px;">Biomes</h4>
<ul style="list-style-type: square; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li>'''The Wood Plains:''' The most common biome in the dimension. Flat, open, and deceptively quiet.</li>
    <li>'''The Thicket:''' A heavily packed region containing wooden trees. No geodes generate beneath the surface here.</li>
    <li>'''The Deep Backwoods:''' A dense area filled with taller tree trunks. It is constructed out of spruce wood variants instead of oak, and rotten geodes generate far more frequently here.</li>
</ul>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE BACKWOODS - IMMUNE SYSTEM
     ========================================== -->
<h4 style="color: #ffccbc; margin-top: 25px; margin-bottom: 10px; font-size: 1.25em;">Dimension Immune System & Spore Threat</h4>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">[[dimensions|The Backwoods dimension]] is a living, hyper-defensive ecosystem. It possesses a highly coordinated, autonomous defense system, the <strong>Immune System</strong>, specifically engineered to seek out, contain, and purge external biological contamination, such as the parasitic fungal outbreaks from the <strong>Spore</strong> mod. When Spore infestations cross critical thresholds inside the Backwoods, the dimension's defense network responds by summoning its ultimate eradication sentry: <strong>The Rot</strong>.</p>

<div style="background: rgba(30, 20, 20, 0.4); border: 1px solid #3e2723; border-left: 4px solid #d84315; padding: 16px; margin: 20px 0; color: #e0dcd3; font-size: 0.9em; border-radius: 4px;">
<strong style="color: #ff7043; text-transform: uppercase; font-size: 0.85em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; display: block; margin-bottom: 4px;">Universal Spore Target Alignment:</strong> The Backwoods immune system triggers defenses exclusively in response to entities belonging to the <code>spore</code> namespace. Normal mod entities (including other hostile vanilla/modded monsters and peaceful residents) do not raise the dimension's threat level.
</div>

<h5 style="color: #ffccbc; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">Threat Accumulation & Decay</h5>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">The dimension actively tracks a global <strong>Spore Threat (0 to 100 range)</strong> on every active player. Threat levels rise dynamically based on biological markers, activity, and direct combat nearby:</p>
<ul style="margin: 0 0 20px 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: square; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em; color: #c8c4b7;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Passive Scanning:</strong> Every second (20 ticks), the dimension scans a 128-block radius around the player. Each <strong>Standard Spore Entity</strong> (max HP &lt; 150) adds <strong>+1 threat</strong>. Each <strong>Strong Spore Entity</strong> (max HP &ge; 150) adds <strong>+5 threat</strong> to the player's persistent registry.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Combat Friction:</strong> Engaging in combat with Spore entities (either a Spore entity taking damage or inflicting damage on another creature) releases airborne threat markers, adding <strong>+2 threat</strong> to all players within a 128-block radius.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>Fatality Escalation:</strong> The death of a Spore entity, or a Spore entity killing a creature, triggers severe alarms, instantly adding <strong>+5 threat</strong> to all proximal players.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong>Natural Decay:</strong> When no Spore entities are nearby to sustain the contamination, the threat decays naturally at a rate of <strong>1 point per second (20 ticks)</strong>.</li>
</ul>

<h5 style="color: #ffccbc; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">Warnings & Acoustic Resonance (The Bell Toll)</h5>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">Before mounting a physical response, the dimension communicates its extreme rejection of the alien biomass. When a Rot sentinel is primed to deploy, the affected player is met with an ominous, red on-screen warning.</p>

<h5 style="color: #ffccbc; margin-top: 20px; margin-bottom: 8px; font-size: 1.05em; font-weight: 500;">Sentinel Activation Protocol</h5>
<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">The Immune System triggers physical pruning (summoning <strong>The Rot</strong>) via two distinct activation criteria:</p>
<ul style="margin: 0 0 20px 0; padding-left: 18px; list-style-type: disc; line-height: 1.7; font-size: 0.9em; color: #c8c4b7;">
    <li style="margin-bottom: 8px;"><strong>The Strong Sentry Trigger:</strong> If a player's Spore Threat level is <strong>&ge; 40</strong> (or immediately in Creative Mode) and at least one <strong>Strong Spore Entity</strong> (HP &ge; 150) is detected within 128 blocks, a summon is initiated.</li>
    <li style="margin-bottom: 0;"><strong>The Fungal Mass Swarm Trigger:</strong> If a massive cluster of <strong>Standard Spore Entities</strong> (HP &lt; 150) accumulates nearby, the dimension initiates a mass-based trigger. Once there are <strong>&ge; 25 small spores</strong> nearby, a probability check is performed every 10 ticks. The chance scales linearly from a baseline <strong>1% (at 25 spores) up to a guaranteed 50% (at 100+ spores)</strong> per check to deploy defenses.</li>
</ul>

<div style="background: rgba(30, 20, 20, 0.3); border-left: 3px solid #d84315; padding: 18px 20px; margin-bottom: 20px; font-size: 0.9em; color: #e0dcd3; line-height: 1.7; border-radius: 0 4px 4px 0;">
<strong style="color: #ff7043; text-transform: uppercase; font-size: 0.85em; letter-spacing: 0.05em; display: block; margin-bottom: 4px;">Dynamic Aerial Deployment:</strong>
When deployed, the Rot does not simply walk into battle. It is synthesized high up in the atmosphere before crashing down.
</div>

<p style="margin-bottom: 15px; font-size: 0.95em;">To prevent excessive entity clustering and performance degradation, there is a strict, per-player summon cooldown of <strong>10 minutes (12,000 ticks)</strong> before the dimension can materialize another Rot sentinel (reduced to 30 seconds for creative mode testing).</p>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE LOSS
     ========================================== -->
<h3 style="color: #90caf9; margin-top: 35px; font-size: 1.5em; border-bottom: 1px solid #1565c0; padding-bottom: 3px;">The Loss</h3>
<p>The Loss is a sub-dimension that players are pulled into involuntarily after spending too long in the Backwoods; it cannot be entered willingly. It resembles a world that has forgotten what it was, featuring an ash-grey sky and terrain covered entirely in pale, washed-out blocks. Some areas appear partially infected by plaque that slowly spreads, as if the world's memory of itself is failing. Spending time within this realm may permanently alter your inventory items.</p>

<h4 style="color: #d7ccc8; margin-top: 15px;">Exits</h4>
<p>You can either make a portal back since the igniters success rates vary by dimension or wait for the dimension to kick you out since you have a timer of 20 minutes.</p>
<h4 style="color: #d7ccc8; margin-top: 15px;">Entrance</h4>
<p>You can only enter this dimension involuntarily by being on the last stage of [[Sanity|mental degradation]] with a chance of <strong>0.2666% per second</strong>.</p>

<h4 style="color: #d7ccc8; margin-top: 15px;">Fading Mechanics</h4>
<p>The Loss actively drains the physical substance of living beings. Simply being inside The Loss—or holding a Faded Block anywhere in your inventory—causes your entity to undergo <strong>Existential Phase-Shifting</strong> (accumulating a "fade level").</p>
<ul style="list-style-type: square; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li><strong>Visual Phasing:</strong> As you fade, your body (including skins, armor, capes, and held items) becomes increasingly translucent and begins to emit a ghostly shimmer. Ash particles will constantly shed from your form.</li>
    <li><strong>Physical Deterioration:</strong> Your attack damage and gravity are dynamically reduced the more you fade, making your strikes weaker and your jumps floaty.</li>
    <li><strong>Intangibility:</strong> Once your fade level surpasses a critical threshold (15.0), you become partially intangible—all incoming projectiles (like arrows) will phase harmlessly right through you.</li>
</ul>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE ROTTING
     ========================================== -->
<h3 style="color: #ba68c8; margin-top: 35px; font-size: 1.5em; border-bottom: 1px solid #7b1fa2; padding-bottom: 3px;">The Rotting</h3>
<p>The Rotting sub-dimension is a dark, atmospheric realm characterized by its dense, towering forests and a perpetual, suffocating fog. The dimension is made of rotting oak wood, features abundant ground foliage like mushrooms and dead bushes, and is densely populated with 32-block-high trees. The atmosphere is oppressive; the fog reduces visibility significantly, forcing players to rely on artificial light sources to navigate the dense pillars of wood.</p>

<h4 style="color: #e1bee7; margin-top: 15px; font-size: 1.1em;">Biomes</h4>
<ul style="list-style-type: square; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li>'''Rotting Deep:''' The single, defining biome of the dimension. Constructed of rotting oak wood with abundant ground foliage like mushrooms and dead bushes, and densely populated with 32-block-high trees.</li>
</ul>

<div style="background: rgba(35, 25, 35, 0.5); border: 1px solid #7b1fa2; border-radius: 6px; padding: 15px; margin-top: 15px;">
* '''Entry Requirement:''' This dimension can only be accessed by finding and entering a geode in [[dimensions|The Backwoods dimension]].
* '''Environmental Rules:''' There is no day/night cycle, and the entire dimension consists solely of the Rotting Deep biome. Vines harvested in the Rotting carry a chance to drop string.
</div>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE GRAIN
     ========================================== -->
<h3 style="color: #fff59d; margin-top: 35px; font-size: 1.5em; border-bottom: 1px solid #fbc02d; padding-bottom: 3px;">The Grain</h3>
<p>Characterized by a thick yellow fog, this dimension is constructed purely out of oak planks. The Grain can be entered by getting struck by a Splinter, building up to high altitudes, or simply staying in the Backwoods for an extended duration.</p>

<div style="background: rgba(40, 40, 20, 0.15); border-left: 4px solid #fbc02d; padding: 12px; margin-bottom: 20px; border-radius: 4px; font-size: 0.95em;">
'''Version Note:''' Some architectural features within this dimension are currently available on NeoForge 1.21.x builds only.
</div>

<h4 style="color: #fff9c4; margin-bottom: 10px;">Biomes</h4>
<ul style="list-style-type: square; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li>'''The Uniform Grain:''' The vast plains of the dimension. It features flat expanses and completely lacks tree growth.</li>
    <li>'''The Stillwood:''' The primary forest sector of the dimension, densely packed with wooden trees.</li>
    <li>'''The Splinter Nest:''' A haunting, high-density region characterized by colossal, highly repetitive wooden frameworks.</li>
    <li>'''The Pillar Thicket:''' A rare and unsettling architectural anomaly found deep within the realm. It forms a vertical labyrinth composed of colossal, hollow wooden towers.</li>
    <li>'''The Labyrinthine Grids:''' A specialized biome utilizing a unique Procedural Feature to generate a massive, ever-changing obstacle course of towering walls. Because the maze generates procedurally per chunk, traditional mapping layouts will not work, forcing players to rely on spatial awareness.</li>
    <li>'''The Fractured Barrens:''' A vast landscape of towering, fractured wooden growths that stretch into the sky. Its tree structures split into unnatural, angular branches that resemble broken frameworks rather than natural forests, creating massive silhouettes fading into the distant fog.</li>
</ul>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE FAMILIAR
     ========================================== -->
<h3 style="color: #a5d6a7; margin-top: 35px; font-size: 1.5em; border-bottom: 1px solid #2e7d32; padding-bottom: 3px;">The Familiar</h3>
<p>The Familiar acts as a simulated, false version of the Overworld spanning seven distinct biomes. The longer you stay, the more you will notice that everything here is actively watching you. Mobs do not behave as they should; they do not run, they do not feed. Instead, they simply turn, look, and wait. Entities here will stare continuously at the player, play ambient sounds when watched for too long, and carry a chance to vanish instantly when attacked. There is no obvious way out.</p>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE PETRIFIED WEALD
     ========================================== -->
<h3 style="color: #b0bec5; margin-top: 35px; font-size: 1.5em; border-bottom: 1px solid #455a64; padding-bottom: 3px;">The Petrified Weald</h3>
<p>The ancient, calcified remains of a world long dead. Everything within is either calcified or rotting, trapped under a permanent, heavy charcoal fog. Fire is highly unstable here, flickering out the moment it is left unattended.</p>

<div style="background: rgba(30, 35, 40, 0.5); border: 1px solid #455a64; border-radius: 6px; padding: 15px; margin-bottom: 20px;">
'''Required Portal Frame Material:''' Petrified Rotten Oak Wood
</div>

<h4 style="color: #cfd8dc; margin-bottom: 10px;">Biomes</h4>
<ul style="list-style-type: square; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li>'''Calcified Plains:''' A haunting, desolate wasteland where vegetation has petrified. The landscape is dominated by massive, jagged spikes of petrified wood that lean precariously toward the west, often reaching heights of 100 blocks.</li>
    <li>'''Weald Outskirts:''' The youngest part of the forest, sparsely populated with Fancy Oak style trees and scattered jungle bushes.</li>
    <li>'''Petrified Thickwoods:''' A dense, claustrophobic forest of Dark Oak and Mega Spruce style trees. This serves as the primary hunting ground for Splinters.</li>
    <li>'''Fossilized Core:''' The ancient heart of the dimension featuring massive Mega Jungles style trees. This is the exclusive generation site where Ancient Debris can be found embedded directly within petrified wood layers.</li>
    <li>'''Ashen Barrens:''' A desolate field of ash roses with a deepslate floor. Entering this biome inflicts the '''Heavy Lungs''' condition, reducing your movement speed, oxygen bonus, mining efficiency, and attack speed for as long as you remain inside.</li>
</ul>

<!-- ==========================================
     THE SUB-STRATA
     ========================================== -->
<h3 style="color: #bcaaa4; margin-top: 35px; font-size: 1.5em; border-bottom: 1px solid #5d4037; padding-bottom: 3px;">The Sub-Strata</h3>
<p>An oppressive extension of the vertical layers beneath the surface. Deeply connected to the architectural themes of The Grain, the Sub-Strata shifts the primary environmental palette entirely to oak planks while maintaining the characteristic yellow fog.</p>

<h4 style="color: #d7ccc8; margin-top: 15px;">Access & Entry Mechanics</h4>
<p>Unlike standard dimensions that utilize structural gates or item triggers, entry into the Sub-Strata is strictly terminal: '''a player can only slip into this dimension by dying while physically inside The Grain.'''</p>
<p>Upon crossing the threshold, an entry procedure forces the player to spawn at the highest possible point in the world geometry (Y=319). Players must navigate downward, descending through the massive Scaffolding Tower structure to reach the main environment at Y=161.</p>

<h4 style="color: #d7ccc8; margin-top: 15px;">Biomes & Generation</h4>
<ul style="list-style-type: square; padding-left: 20px;">
    <li>'''The Dead Grain:''' A desolate, monochrome variation of standard Grain architecture. It acts as the secondary habitat for Blindspot Splinters. Their local safety exclusion zones decay dynamically over time, shrinking from a massive 120 blocks down to a suffocating 16 blocks the longer a player survives inside. May cause performance problems due to [[structures]].</li>
</ul>
<p>The architecture of this dimension is structured around repetitive geometric configurations and forgotten ruins. For a full breakdown of specific layouts and procedural grids, see the official documentation at [[Structures#The_Sub_Strata|Structures: The Sub-Strata]].</p>
